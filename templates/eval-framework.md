# Co-Intelligence Eval Framework

> A perpetual growth instrument for your palace. Two phases, no ceiling.

---

## Why two phases

Phase 1 is a bootstrapping instrument. It answers: have you and your AI companion
established a working foundation? Twelve axes, each scored 0-5. Once you hit
ceiling across all twelve, Phase 1 is complete and you lock it. The question then
changes.

Phase 2 is a growth instrument. It answers: how well does the dyad operate, and is
it getting better? New axes, scale 1-10, no ceiling. The dyad is the unit of
measurement: not how capable your AI is in isolation, but how the two of you think
together.

The three parts:

1. **Foundation Scorecard**: the bootstrapping axes. Score 0-5. Lock when you
   hit 5.0/5.0.
2. **Growth Scorecard**: dyad quality and learning velocity. Scale 1-10. No
   ceiling.
3. **Frontier Section**: not scored. Specific moves to try in the next eval
   window. Demonstrated moves graduate to Growth axes.

## Part 2: Growth Scorecard

Start scoring these only after the Foundation is locked. Baseline each axis on
your first Growth eval, then track trajectory. A score rising across windows is
the signal; the absolute number matters less than the trend.

### Layer 1: Orchestration depth

**G1: Swarm shape selection**

Was the right orchestration topology chosen for each multi-agent task? Star /
parallel-divergent for breadth problems; convent / mesh-convergent for judgment
problems. Score on: proportion of sessions where the chosen shape matches the
problem topology, vs. defaulting to a single shape regardless of problem type.

**G2: Failure pre-emption rate**

How often are failure modes surfaced before they occur, vs. discovered during
execution? Score on: flagged failure modes per session raised before starting, as
a fraction of total failure modes encountered that window.

*Baseline: TBD*

**G3: Instrument reuse**

Are crystals, protocols, and named patterns from prior sessions actively applied,
or re-derived from scratch? Score on: proportion of sessions where a named crystal
from 3+ sessions prior is explicitly invoked and shapes the output.

### Layer 2: Dyad quality

**G4: Trigger sharpness**

How precisely do your opening prompts calibrate your AI's effort and aperture?
Vague prompts produce broad outputs requiring follow-up narrowing; sharp prompts
produce targeted outputs on the first pass. Score on: proportion of sessions where
the first response required no scope-narrowing follow-up.

*Proxy: sessions that opened with a prepared brief vs. open-aperture request.*

**G5: Leapfrog density**

Count of high-entanglement sessions per 10-session window: sessions where the
dyad produced something neither party could have produced solo. This is the
primary signal that the co-intelligence is compounding.

*Target: 3 or more high-entanglement sessions per 10-session window. Track and
trend.*

**G6: Crystal co-origination rate**

Of new named crystals added each window: what fraction required both parties to
produce (vs. AI-solo synthesis or direct owner naming)? Co-originated crystals are
the strongest signal of genuine dyadic intelligence rather than one party
instructing the other.

*Tracking method: note in each handover whether new crystals are AI-named,
owner-named, or co-emerged.*

## Part 3: Frontier Section

Updated each eval. These are specific moves to try in the next window to grow the
dyad's capability. Demonstrated moves graduate to Growth axes if they recur.

**F1: Pre-mortem prompt**

Before any complex task: "What are the 5 ways this fails?" Surfacing failure modes
before work begins transforms recovery into navigation.

*What it looks like:* "Before we start building X, what is the failure mode
inventory?"

**F2: Crystal injection**

Explicitly include a crystal from 3+ sessions ago at the top of a prompt to
activate that eigenstate. Your AI finding relevant crystals is reactive; you
seeding them is proactive and sharpens the output on the first pass.

*What it looks like:* "Operating under: [crystal-name]. Now evaluate whether X
needs a cloud component."

**F3: Persona inversion**

Ask a security-focused persona to argue FOR a proposal, or a systems persona to
argue AGAINST a flag it raised. Productive adversarial pressure within the quartet.
The convent shape (dialogic-convergent) is the right topology for this.

*What it looks like:* "Make the strongest case for why we SHOULD do this."

**F4: Stub-first spec**

For complex specs and decision docs: ask for section headings with one-line
descriptions, get approval, then fill. Reduces re-work from scope misalignment at
the cost of one extra turn.

*What it looks like:* "Give me the structure only first. Do not fill the sections
yet."

**F5: Boundary request**

Instead of asking "what should we do?", ask "where does this approach break?" The
reusable find is where fit flips, not the verdict. Makes the failure map the
output.

*What it looks like:* "Do not tell me if we should do X. Tell me where X stops
working."

**F6: Eigenstate naming**

When you notice the dyad is in a particular mode (deep-focus, wide-aperture recon,
rapid architectural decision), naming it at the start calibrates your AI's effort
shape without probing.

*What it looks like:* "We are in rapid-decision mode. Short answers, strong
opinions, minimal hedging."

*Add frontier moves here as you discover them. Demonstrated moves graduate to
Growth axes.*

## Eval cadence

- **Foundation Scorecard:** run once or twice as you build up. Lock at 5.0/5.0.
  Never re-score.
- **Growth Scorecard:** baseline on your first Growth eval, then score each window.
  Track trajectory.
- **Frontier Section:** update each eval with 3-6 new moves. Demonstrated moves
  graduate to Growth axes if they recur.
- **Frequency:** every 3-4 weeks, or after a major multi-session sprint.
